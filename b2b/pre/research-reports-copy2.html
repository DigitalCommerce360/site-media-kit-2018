Best practices in manufacturing B2B e-commerce FEBRUARY

They are manufacturers in diverse markets but companies such as 3M, Nike, Ford, Dell, IBM and Boeing among others share a common trait when it comes to business-to-business e-commerce. Namely they are all adept at building and operating successful e-commerce sites that generate online sales and provide great customer service to a highly demanding and specialized audience of industrial, educational and government buyers. In this report to be published in January 2018, B2BecNews looks in-depth at the business strategies, technology deployment, marketing and customer service best practices of leading manufacturers selling online, and the lessons for other manufacturing companies.

Wholesalers and distributors take a holistic view approach to B2B e-commerce APRIL
The internet is disrupting wholesaling, and wholesalers and distributors are investing in new e-commerce technology to meet that challenge. But like the manufacturers that supply them, wholesalers and distributors need to get beyond competing on price and availability, and deliver an excellent online customer experience that includes deep product information, convenient payment options and integration into customers’ accounting systems. What’s more, these companies need to take full advantage of fast-growing online marketplaces like Amazon Business, eBay, Walmart.com and Newegg Business. There are 300,000 wholesalers in the U.S. but 50 of the largest companies generate 25% of all industry revenue, says the U.S. Small Business Administration. How fast are wholesalers and distributors making the transition to B2B e-commerce and which ones are moving the fastest? In its report to be published in March 2018, B2BecNews analyzes the trends, technology and business practices driving wholesale e-commerce forward and highlights which wholesalers and distributors are ahead of the game.

Best Practices in Web Procurement JULY
Web-based procurement, commonly referred to as e-procurement, is a B2B e-commerce technology that was once the back-office domain of supply chain managers, information technology directors and purchasing agents. It is now a front-and-center corporate function used by personnel throughout an organization. A 2017 survey Deloitte finds that 75% of executives believe that web procurement will play a much larger role in their company’s overall digital strategy going forward. But how effective are the latest e-procurement software applications and networks in accomplishing these goals? In its forthcoming report---Best Practices in Web Procurement—B2BecNews will analyze current technology and business trends and delivers practical and hands-on data, research and strategies companies of all sizes can use to improve their supply chain and procurement cycle management.

B2B E-Commerce 300 SEPTEMBER
The business-to-business e-commerce market is more than double the size of the B2C market, and it’s also a lot more complicated and fragmented. The B2B E-Commerce 300 is the only publication of its kind that seeks to measure this market, collect vital data on traffic and conversion rates, and identify the companies leading the charge in changing the way businesses operate. The report includes a ranking of the 300 largest manufacturers, distributors, wholesalers and retailers bringing in big sales online.

Automotive: A B2B e-commerce market hits the fast lane OCTOBER
More so than many other industries, automotive manufacturers, suppliers and dealers were quick to embrace B2B e-commerce. Virtually all major vehicle manufacturers have an e-commerce program in place electronically connecting them to networks of suppliers, dealers and customers. E-commerce sales in the U.S. automotive parts market is expected to account for about 11%, or $5.06 billion, of total sales of $46 billion in 2017, according to automotive e-commerce technology developer Sana. Automotive purchasing agents also use commerce-enabled networks and portals to make more informed buying decisions. This report to be published in February 2018 analyzes and sizes up the automotive B2B e-commerce market and highlights which manufacturers, suppliers and dealers are putting e-commerce into over drive while others remain stuck on the side of the road with outdated supply chains and sales channels.
